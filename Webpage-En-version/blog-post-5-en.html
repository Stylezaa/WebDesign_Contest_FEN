Importance of education for economic development


From an economic point of view, education is a fundamental factor in the country's economic development. No country would be sustainable without large investments in human capital. Education increases people's productivity and creativity. In addition, it also plays an important role in maintaining economic and social progress and improving income distribution. Education is one of the best tools for reducing poverty, inequality, and a cornerstone for sustained economic growth. The increased economic return is due to the increased education and skills of employees. Employees can work more efficiently, how much productivity will be greater and the economy will be successful. Therefore, since employees are able to operate a business has an advantage in competitive differentiation, education is a key factor in economic development. While other factors are added, hiring educated and skilled employees already has its advantages.
